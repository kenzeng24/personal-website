From H&E histology to patient-level prognosis

Our model processes gigapixel H&E slides — extracting patches, encoding morphological features with a pathology foundation model, and aggregating them into a patient-level risk prediction.

RESEARCH METHODS

The core technical challenges are learning from censored outcomes, estimating treatment benefit without randomized data, and producing calibrated predictions that generalize across institutions.

Survival analysis

Deep survival models trained on right-censored time-to-event labels, preserving the full event-time distribution rather than collapsing to binary outcomes.

Multimodal fusion

Patch embeddings from a pathology foundation model are aggregated via multiple instance learning and fused with structured clinical variables to produce a patient-level risk score.

Causal inference

Individualized treatment benefit estimated from observational cohorts using deep learning-based adjustment, without relying on randomized trial data.

Calibration

Post-hoc recalibration aligns predicted risk scores with observed recurrence rates across institutions, so that a predicted 20% risk means 20% in practice.

RESEARCH HIGHLIGHTS

Validated across 5 external cohorts, our model produces statistically separable survival curves with a 2.84x difference in recurrence rate between risk groups.
